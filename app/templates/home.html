Platform features

Everything you need in a crisis

Built for first responders, volunteers, and affected communities to coordinate rapidly.

🗺️

Live disaster map

Interactive Leaflet.js map showing all active disasters with severity-coded markers and popup details.

🤖

ML severity prediction

Random Forest model automatically predicts severity (low → critical) based on type, location, and population affected.

🔔

Email & SMS alerts

Instantly notify emergency contacts via email or SMS when a crisis is reported or escalates.

🌤️

Weather integration

Live OpenWeatherMap data on every crisis — temperature, wind speed, and humidity at the disaster location.

📊

Dashboard analytics

Chart.js visualisations — events by type, severity breakdown, and 7-day activity timeline.

🙋

Volunteer coordination

Register volunteers per disaster, track skills and availability, manage approval status.

📦

Resource management

Submit, track, and fulfil resource requests and relief supply inventories per crisis.

🚗

Evacuation routes

Define and monitor evacuation corridors with live status — open, congested, or closed.

🔒

Security & audit logs

Every action logged with IP and timestamp. CSRF, rate limiting, bcrypt hashing, role-based access.

How it works

From report to response in minutes

1

Report a crisis

Submit a disaster report with location, type, and details. ML instantly predicts severity.

2

Alerts go out

Emergency contacts notified instantly via email or SMS. Responders mobilised.

3

Coordinate response

Track affected areas, request resources, assign volunteers, log the incident timeline.

4

Monitor & resolve

Update status, post announcements, and mark the crisis resolved when contained.

What to do when a flash flood warning is issued

Flash floods develop within minutes. Evacuate low-lying areas, avoid driving through water, and locate high ground. Even 6 inches of moving water can knock a person down.

Building defensible space around your home

Creating a 30–100 foot buffer zone significantly reduces wildfire risk. Clear dry vegetation, use fire-resistant plants, and maintain ember-resistant vents.

Build your 72-hour emergency kit

Every household needs a go-bag: 3 days of water (1 gallon/person/day), non-perishable food, first aid kit, flashlight, batteries, copies of documents, and cash.
