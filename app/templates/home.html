Platform features

Everything you need in a crisis

Built for first responders, volunteers, and affected communities to coordinate rapidly and effectively.

🗺️

Live disaster map

Interactive Leaflet.js map showing all active disasters in real time with severity-coded markers and popup details.

🤖

ML severity prediction

Machine learning model automatically predicts disaster severity (low → critical) based on type, location, and affected population.

🔔

Email & SMS alerts

Instantly notify emergency contacts via email or SMS when a new crisis is reported or severity escalates.

🌤️

Weather integration

Live weather data from OpenWeatherMap displayed on every crisis report — temperature, wind speed, and humidity.

📊

Dashboard charts

Visual analytics with Chart.js — events by type, severity breakdown, and 7-day activity timeline.

🙋

Volunteer coordination

Register volunteers for specific disasters, track their skills and availability, and manage their status.

📦

Resource management

Request, track, and fulfil relief supplies and resource requests tied to each crisis report.

🚗

Evacuation routes

Define and monitor evacuation routes with real-time status — open, congested, or closed.

🔒

Security & audit logs

Every action is logged with IP address and timestamp. CSRF protection, rate limiting, and bcrypt password hashing.

How it works

From report to response in minutes

1

Report a crisis

Any registered user can submit a disaster report with location, type, and details. The ML model instantly predicts severity.

2

Alerts go out

Emergency contacts are notified instantly via email or SMS. Responders are mobilised.

3

Coordinate response

Track affected areas, request resources, assign volunteers, and log the incident timeline — all in one place.

4

Monitor & resolve

Update status, post announcements, and mark the crisis resolved when the situation is under control.

Flood

What to do when a flash flood warning is issued

Flash floods can develop within minutes. Learn the immediate steps — evacuate low-lying areas, avoid driving through water, and locate high ground. Even 6 inches of moving water can knock a person down.

Earthquake

Drop, Cover, and Hold On — the science behind it

The "Drop, Cover, Hold On" method remains the most effective way to protect yourself during shaking. Research from earthquake engineering studies shows it reduces injury risk by over 70% compared to running outside.

Wildfire

Building defensible space around your home

Creating a buffer zone of 30–100 feet around structures significantly reduces wildfire risk. This includes clearing dry vegetation, using fire-resistant plants, and maintaining ember-resistant vents and gutters.

Prevention guide Read more →
Preparedness

Build your 72-hour emergency kit

Every household should have a go-bag ready: 3 days of water (1 gallon per person per day), non-perishable food, a first aid kit, flashlight, batteries, copies of documents, and cash. Customise for your family's needs.

How climate change is intensifying natural disasters

Rising global temperatures are making hurricanes stronger, wildfires more frequent, and floods more severe. Understanding the link between climate change and disaster frequency helps communities plan and adapt better.

The Incident Command System (ICS) explained

ICS is the standardised approach used by emergency responders worldwide. It establishes a clear chain of command, unified operations, and resource management — preventing chaos in the critical first hours of a disaster.
